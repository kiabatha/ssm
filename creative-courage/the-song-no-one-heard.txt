The Song No One Heard!

Mateo had a song in his heart. It was a happy, bubbling tune that he had made up himself. He would hum it while walking to school and tap its rhythm on his desk. He loved his secret song. But sharing it? That was a terrifying thought.

The school talent show was coming up. His friends were signing up to tell jokes or do magic tricks. "You should play your song, Mateo!" they said.

The idea made his palms sweat. What if everyone thought it was silly? What if he messed up and the notes came out wrong? His song felt safe and perfect inside his head. Letting it out felt dangerous.

On the day of the sign-ups, his feet felt stuck to the floor. But then he thought, What if my song could make someone else happy, like it makes me happy? That thought was a little spark of courage. He walked to the sign-up sheet and, with a trembling hand, wrote his name.

The night of the show, his knees knocked together as he walked onto the big, bright stage. He looked out at the blur of faces, took a deep breath, and began to play. At first, his fingers fumbled, but then he found the rhythm. He played his happy, bubbling tune. When he finished, there was a moment of silence, and then the room erupted in applause. A younger girl in the front row was smiling a huge, genuine smile.
