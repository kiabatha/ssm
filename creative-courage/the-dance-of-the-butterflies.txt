The Dance of the Butterflies!

In Zara's ballet class, all the other girls seemed to float like feathers. When they danced, their movements were sharp, precise, and exactly what the teacher demonstrated. But when Zara danced, she felt the music differently. It made her want to spin with her arms wide open, to leap as if she were flying, to tell a story without words.

The teacher would gently correct her. "Arms in third position, Zara. Point your toes like the others."
Zara felt clumsy and out of place. Maybe she just wasn't a good dancer. She thought about quitting. It was easier to stop than to keep being different.

But her body loved to move. At home, in her garden, she would dance like the butterfliesâ€”fluttering, unpredictable, and free. It felt like joy.

For the class recital, the routine was a strict, traditional dance. As the music started, Zara tried her best to follow the steps. But when the violins swelled, her heart swelled with them. For a moment, she forgot the strict steps. She spun, her arms creating wide, beautiful circles, her face looking up to the sky. It was just one move, a moment of pure, brave, her.

After the performance, a woman she didn't know came up to her. "Your dance was so beautiful and full of feeling," she said. "You danced like you meant it." The other dancers had been perfect, but Zara had been memorable. She learned that sometimes, creative courage means adding your own unique voice to the song, even if it's different from everyone else's.
